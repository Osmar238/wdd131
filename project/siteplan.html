Site Plan: Panadería La Joya

Osmar Arevalo

WDD 131

Site Name

Panadería La Joya

I chose this name because it is the actual established name of my uncle's family business. Keeping the real name is essential for brand consistency so local customers can recognize the bakery immediately.

Site Purpose & Language Strategy

The purpose of this website is to establish a digital presence for "Panadería La Joya". It will allow the local community to easily view our catalog of fresh breads (sweet and salty), check our daily baking schedule, and find our location.

Target Audience & Language:
The primary audience consists of local customers in Tamaulipas, Mexico. To ensure the site is fully accessible to the community and usable by the client (my uncle, the bakery owner), the website content and interface will be presented entirely in Spanish.

This decision supports the project's usability goals by tailoring the user experience (UX) to the specific needs of the native Spanish-speaking customer base and the business management.

Scenarios

Questions my target audience will ask:

  • "What types of sweet bread (conchas, donas, etc.) do you have available right now?"
  • "Do you accept orders for special events or large quantities?"

Color Schema

I selected warm, earth-toned colors to represent the natural ingredients and the warmth of fresh bread.

Typography

Heading Font: Merriweather

Body Font: Open Sans - This is a clean, readable font for the main content of the website, ensuring that descriptions of our delicious bread are easy to read on any device.
